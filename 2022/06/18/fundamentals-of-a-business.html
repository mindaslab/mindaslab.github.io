Fundamentals of a Business

Jun 18, 2022

I was talking with my friend who wanted to have his own business. I soon found out that he wanted to have a business to get rich, to leave something for his kids, which means his business has failed even before it materialized. He never talked about the three main things which I think is essential for a business.

In this blog I want to write what qualities I think are fundamental for a business, what qualities I think that will make it successful.

Value

The first fundamental thing is value. Some one wants to buy your product or service only if they think itâ€™s valuable, or else they have to be a fool to invest money. No matter how ever less value it is, your business must add value.

For example I was consulting for a company called DataMaking, our team size grew a lot, we had to do something to keep meeting times low, so we developed a tool doneit. It reduced our meeting times. After it worked for us, we told our friends to try, gave them free trials and some of them became paid subscribers.

There was some value for a tool creation, we could identify it and so we created it. It later became a business.

Honesty

Honesty is necessary for business. Say you have a bank backing you, they want to know how your business will go so that they can predict the future and make sure there is enough funds available to the creditors. If you are dishonest, they will not be able to help you in the long run and will loose trust in you.

If you are not honest about the product or service you are selling to someone, people will loose trust in it. For example almost no startups use Oracle, no programmer uses Windows. People have lost trust in them.
